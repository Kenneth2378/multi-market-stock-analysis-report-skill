# Research Depth Gate

Use this checklist before drafting. It prevents a polished but shallow or company-mismatched report.

## Minimum evidence

| Area | Required evidence |
|---|---|
| Identity | Company, ticker, exchange, board, currency, trading/risk status |
| Earnings | Annual and latest quarterly revenue, attributable profit, adjusted profit, gross margin, and YoY calculations |
| Cash quality | Operating cash flow and its relationship to profit; explain working-capital effects |
| Balance sheet | Assets, equity, liabilities, debt ratio, receivables, inventory, financing, and material impairment where available |
| Valuation | Current multi-metric valuation, metric basis, historical context, peers, and growth match |
| Price/volume | Three-month return/high/low, MA20/MA60, volume/turnover, high-volume days, support/resistance, and interpretation |
| Catalysts | At least two dated, material company or industry events with impact mechanism |
| Judgment | Three-part evidence chain, scenarios, observation conditions, invalidation conditions, and methodology |

When reliable evidence is unavailable, state the gap and use `NEEDS_REVIEW`; do not replace evidence with boilerplate.

## Company-specific writing test

For every analytical paragraph, ask: if the company name were replaced with another ticker, would the paragraph still read naturally?

- If yes, add company-specific metrics, products, subsidiaries, customers, announcements, or price structures.
- If the paragraph remains generic after one revision, remove it.

Use the company profile only for identity and business description. Use filings and announcements for financial and event conclusions.

## Material-announcement test

For each important announcement, explain:

1. What changed.
2. Which financial or strategic driver it affects.
3. Expected timing.
4. What evidence would confirm or invalidate the impact.

Listing announcement titles without analysis does not pass.

## Scenario test

Each scenario must contain measurable triggers. Suitable triggers include revenue/profit growth, margin, operating cash flow, receivables/inventory, valuation range, price structure, turnover, regulatory decision, transaction closing, or product shipment.

Do not use generic `demand improves` or `valuation contracts` without target-company evidence.

## Semantic mismatch examples

Fail the report if any of these occur:

- Wrong exchange or board.
- `ST/*ST` language for a normal stock, or missing risk-warning language for an `ST/*ST` stock.
- Wrong industry, product, subsidiary, controlling shareholder, or currency.
- Manufacturing, consumer, finance, or technology boilerplate applied to an unrelated company.
- Risks or catalysts copied from another target.
- Quality status PASS based only on file existence or heading presence.
